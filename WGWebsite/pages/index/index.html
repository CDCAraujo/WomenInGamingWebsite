HOW THE INDUSTRY IS CHANGING

The gaming industry has changed significantly since it first began in the late 1950's, with Physicist William Higinbotham's Tennis for Two. Gaming has gone through many different changes since then. Initially marketed at just boys, it was difficult for women and girls to break into the industry, whether playing, or joining a career. Many barriers have xisted historically for women and girls in the industry, and many are still being fought against today. Changes in marketing strategies, as well as societal changes, have increased the numbers of women and girls in the industry, but much more change is needed before we can see real equality in the industry.
An important element in this change has been the growth of women in streaming, and these women speaking out on their experiences in the industry.
  • Pokimane raised awareness of the double standards in the streaming industry, as well as the vitriol female streamers recieve regarding their looks.
  • Amouranth spoke up about the objectification of girls and women from even as young as childhood. She also spoke up about the dangers women and girls can face from simply declining an invitation from a man
  • Scottish streamers speak up about misogyny in the industry and how they fight against it.

Speaking up about these issues is incedibly important in introducing change, and in encouraging other women to speak out and get involved!

DIFFICULTIES WOMEN FACE IN THE GAMING INDUSTRY

In times past, gaming was a novelty reserved for masculine and tech-savvy men, however the online gaming industry is witnessing the number of women players growing steadily on both consumer and developer level.
Despite not falling short of their male counter parts, this does not mean female gamers are overtly welcomed with open hands, there are quite a few serious issues every female gamer has dealt with.
From sexual harassment to sexism, there is also the issue of gender pay gap and the ignorance of women as a target group of the gaming industry. These and many others pose issues that need to be addressed and communities where women feel safe and welcome are a necessity.

Times are changing though, be part of the change and help us spread awareness and fight these issues!
